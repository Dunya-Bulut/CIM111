STEP 1

Draw the shape with a sketching pencil

Lightly sketch out the shape of the butterfly. Start with the wings and continue with the head. After doing so, add the details in the wings. Remember, you are only sketching. So do not overdo the lines or make your lines bold. You will do it when you feel confident with the general shape and the details. Move to step 2.

STEP 2

Click here to hide step 2

Draw the branches

Now that you are happy with the wings and the details inside, add the branches that the butterfly is standing on. Again, this is still the sketching process so keep your pencil light. Try to make the branches dynamic instead of straight lines. Move on to the next step.

STEP 3

Click here to hide step 3

Add the fine lines with a drawing pen

Now, your whole shape looks like there is a butterfly. You are happy with the overall shape. Change your pencil to an ink pen and loosely track what you draw so far. Do not make bold lines yet. We are going to add the thickness on the next step. For now, just keep it loose and follow the path you drew. Again, do not overdo it. Move on to step 4 to add thickness.

STEP 4

Click here to hide step 4

Bolden specific areas

In this step, you will find the areas that needs thickness. Bold lines indicate depth and texture as well. This is a Monarch Butterfly, so it has a specific design on its wings. It is like fingerprints, they are all different. Try drawing circles first and then fill the rest with black inked pen. This step needs more attention compared to previous steps. Now that you finished this step, you are looking at a line drawing of a butterfly. Add the colors in step 5.

STEP 5

Click here to hide step 5

Add the colors

Use your brush and add lots of water before applying the color to the paper. This will reduce the color pixels per inch and create a soft sky effect. Use a "Azura" blue for sky. Wait few minutes and continue with the wings. Remember, because the second wing is on the back it will have a shadow, thus, it will be a darker color. After adding the colors to the wings, move on to branches and apply 2 more layers after it dries. This will add a great deal of realism because the color change will indicate shadows. You are all done! Congrats, now you know how to draw a butterfly!

DURATION

Drawing time:15 minutes
Watercoloring:3 minutes
Drying time:10 minutes
